RELIEVING SYMPTOMS

1
Saline Rinse
#Sinusitis #Allergy #DryNose #PostNasalDrip

WHY: Washing your nose regularly with saline (salt water) helps to remove allergens, keep your nasal passages moisturised, and aids in tissue repair.

HOW: Buy premixed sachets or make your own saline solution by adding a teaspoon of salt to boiled water. Once cooled, tilt head sideways and use a squeeze bottle or neti pot to gently push the saline through one nostril. Repeat on the other side.
2
Hot Showers
#Sinusitis #Allergy #DryNose

WHY: Warm showers have a 2-for-1 effect for allergy sufferers. The steam helps open up the lungs while moisturising the nasal passages. They also help remove any allergens on the hair and skin picked up during the day.

HOW: The warmer, the better. Opt for a shower with an enclosure to maximis steam intake.
3
Inhaling Steam
#Sinusitis

WHY: As with showers, steam can open up the airways and provide relief - particularly or sinus pain.

HOW: Your weapon of choice may range from a pot over the gas, a kettle, a steam inhaler, or a bowl of just-boiled water and a tea towel. Add eucalyptus or Olbas oil for an additional decongestant effect.
4
Toothpaste
#PostNasalDrip

WHY: Most toothpastes - particularly those with extra menthol - dissolve and help rduce mucus.

HOW: Brushing your teeth first thing in the morning, last at night, and at other inervals can greatly mitigate the impact of post-nasal drip.
5
Chili Peppers Hot
#Sinusitis #DryNose

WHY: Capsaicin, a compound found in chili papers, stimulates the mucous membranes and helps relieve nasal congestion caused by non-allergic rhinitis. It may also facilitate temporary pain relief.

HOW: Simply eating spicy foods can help kickstart-your sinuses and get your nose running. Nasal-sprays containing capsaicin also now exist - although further study is needed to determine their efficacy.

PREVENTING SYMPTOMS

1
Washing Bedclothes
#Allergy

WHY: Dust mites, one of the most common causes of allergic rhinitis, thrive in any type of fabric: particularly sheets.

HOW: Wash bedclothes at a high temperature weekly to both kill dust mites and remove the allergens they produce. Take particularly care to wash pillows: swapping in multiple spare pillow cases each week can greatly reduce overnight blocked nose and sleep apnea.
2
Allergy-Proofing
#Allergy

WHY: Dust mites, as well as animal dander and other allergens, thrive and become concentrated in fabrics of all kinds.

HOW: Swap carpets for hardwood floors, wash curtains semi-regularly at the highest heat possible, and reduce all other fabrics around the home - for example, loose clothes or rugs.
3
Air Filters
#Sinusitis #Allergy

WHY: A high-quality air filter is worth its weight in gold. It can strip all types of free-floating allergens from the air and, if carpets or pets cannot be avoided, it can go a long way to mitigating symptoms.

HOW: Choose a filter with multiple stages. For exapmle: pre-filter (ideal for animal dander), HEPA filter (removes dust and pollen), carbon filter (reduces odours), and UV light unit (kills mold spores).
4
Fitness
#All

WHY: Maintaining a high or even moderate level of aerobic fitness has an outsize influence on allergies and other respiratory issues.

HOW: Walking, jogging, and swimming all have merits depending on your climate. Walking can also aid in relieving symptoms because upright motion allows the sinuses to drain better. Jogging by the seaside is particularly effective at opening and moisturising the airways.
5
Friendly Bacteria
#Sinusitis #Allergy #DryNose

WHY: Recent research suggests having a diverse microbiome (the millions of bacteria in your gut and elsewhere may fortify the immune system and prevent some kinds of infections.

HOW: Kimchi is a natural probiotic (food-containing live bacteria) containing L. Sakei which may may alleviate sinusitis and promote healthful bacteria in the nasal passageways according to several trials. While there are many other different probiotics (containing live bacteria), prebiotics - fibre-containing foods which promote the growth of friendly bacteria - are thought to play the most important role in cultivating a healthy microbiome.
