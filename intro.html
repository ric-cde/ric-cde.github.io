Allergy

Allergic rhinitis is a condition which affects between 10% and 30% of the global population. It typically presents as an inflammation of the nose and is caused by hyper-senstitivity to certain allergenic particles such as dust, pollen, and animal dander.

Sinusitis

Sinusitis is a swelling of the membranes of the sinus cavities which lie behind and above the nose, eyes, and cheeks. It is typically caused by infection from bacterial sources - but other causes such as fungii and allergies have been posited. Symptoms include facial pain, headaches, and nose that is difficult to clear.

Dry Nose

Dryness of the nasal passageways, sometimes referred to as catarrh, can result in thick clumps of dried mucous which adhere to the nasal membranes and cause generalised soreness and irritation. It can often be relieved by a sinus rinse or other form of moisturiser.

Post Nasal Drip

A persistent drip of liquid felt at the back of the throat, this condition is often caused by or related to allergies or sinusitis when excessive mucous is secreted by the mucous membrane. It can lead to further inflammation of tissues in the throat and pharynx.
